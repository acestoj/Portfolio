Sheet 01 / 04 Client: Melbourne Park Year: 2026 Tools: ABAQUS · Fusion 360

SpiderGuide: Universal Chain Guide Attachment

Objective: Melbourne Park required a universal attachment capable of fitting five different electric chain hoists without modifying the existing equipment, while remaining under a strict 5 kg weight limit.

We benchmarked five existing products and translated the client's verbal requirements into measurable engineering criteria. I then led the structural validation of the final design using ABAQUS, evaluating static loading, buckling and vibration performance across the aluminium assembly.

Clamp bracket FEA: stress, plastic strain and displacement (governs design at FOS 2.37×)

Final design: CAD assembly and exploded views

Sheet 02 / 04 Role: Co-Lead Status: In progress 2026 – Present

Establishing Formula SAE at Deakin Burwood

Objective: Formula SAE has not operated at Deakin University's Burwood campus. Establishing a new team requires more than designing a race car. It means earning the confidence of university leadership by demonstrating that the program can operate sustainably, manage risk effectively and remain genuinely student-led, while complementing the existing engineering programs across both campuses.

My role: I co-lead the initiative alongside fellow students, working closely with the technical lead of Deakin's existing motorsport program. Together we're developing the governance framework, implementation roadmap and executive business case, with a strong focus on succession planning, realistic milestones and long-term sustainability.

Progress: We've developed a phased two-year implementation strategy, coordinated stakeholders across both campuses and are preparing the executive proposal for senior leadership. Rather than aiming to build a competitive car immediately, the roadmap prioritises establishing a strong organisational foundation that future student cohorts can continue to build upon.

Sheet 03 / 04 Client: City of Stonnington Year: 2025 Tools: SPACE GASS

Cyclist & Pedestrian Bridge, City of Stonnington

Objective: Design and verify the preliminary structural system for a cable-stayed cyclist and pedestrian bridge for the City of Stonnington. The scope covered modelling the bridge in SPACE GASS, performing independent hand calculations, selecting steel members, designing the footing and validating all software outputs.

Structural Analysis

  • Created a 3D SPACE GASS model of the bridge
  • Applied dead, live and wind loading combinations
  • Determined governing member forces and support reactions

Verification

  • Checked bending, shear and axial capacities by hand
  • Verified footing bearing, sliding and overturning stability
  • Compared hand calculations against software outputs

Design Iteration

  • Updated footing after allowable bearing pressure reduced to 100 kPa
  • Re-checked stability and eccentricity
  • Identified main girder overstress requiring further optimisation

Member selection covered a 900WB257 welded beam for the main girder, 310UB40.4 crossbeams and a 219.1×6.4 mm CHS backstay, all in Grade 300/C350 steel.

Result: The redesigned footing held compliance under the revised geotechnical constraints with comfortable margin. Sheet 04 / 04 Competition: Weir Warman "Project FAMINE" Year: 2024 Tools: Arduino · CAD

Warman Design & Build Competition: Seed Collection & Deposit Machine

Objective: Design and build an autonomous seed collection mechanism for the 2024 Weir Warman "Project FAMINE" competition. The robot was required to autonomously collect and deposit seed pods within a 400 mm cube and complete the task in under 120 seconds. My primary responsibility was the drivetrain and arm mechanism, including CAD design, Arduino control logic, mechanical integration and system testing.

Design specifications

Competition Constraints

  • 400 × 400 × 400 mm starting volume
  • Autonomous operation
  • Single activation
  • 120-second time limit

Hardware

  • Arduino Uno
  • DC gearmotor
  • Servo motor
  • Spur gear drivetrain
  • Laser-cut plywood chassis
  • 3D printed components

Software

  • Arduino IDE
  • Fusion 360

Key engineering decisions

Drivetrain

Designed a spur gear transmission to transfer motor torque directly to the steel drive shaft while maintaining compact packaging within the competition volume.

Arm Mechanism

Implemented servo-controlled positioning with predefined pickup and deposit angles to improve repeatability across different seed heights.

Structural Design

Designed the frame to remain within the competition volume while providing sufficient stiffness for arm operation without adding unnecessary mass.

Integration

Balanced packaging constraints between electronics, drivetrain and collection arm during assembly to ensure all subsystems could operate without interference.
